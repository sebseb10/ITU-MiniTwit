\subsection{Evolution and Refactoring}


Our initial implementation of CI/CD pipelines ran concurently which resulted in us pushing changes to the repository, which at times could crashed the production environment, w/o giving an alert of the downtime to the developers. 
after experiencing this issue we itteratively extended the CD pipeline by adding a check to see if the CI pipeline succeeded before the CD pipeline is triggered.
Further refactorings to the CI/CD pipeline included the use of caches to speed up deployment time which previously has been a bottleneck in the process when trying to make small changes and quickly test them.
\\\\
In early itterations of the program we forgot to cull old containers when updates were pushed, this was found after the implementation of our monitoring where we were able to detect and debug the cause of suspiciously high CPU usage. 
\\
Initially we hosted the monitoring/logging stack on the same droplet as the production environment, which ment that if one went down the other would be affected as well which made it harder to debug the cause of the issues, this was fixed by moving the monitoring/logging stack to a separate droplet.
\\\\
These issues taught us the importance of idempotent deployment, and the importance of monitoring as a tool to quickly introspect, detect and debug issues in production.

\subsection{DevOps Style of Work}

Much more iterative and incremental development process, with a focus on continuous integration and delivery. 
with a larger focus on introspection and adaption to feedback from monitoring and logging, which allowed us to detect and fix issues in production.



This way of working has a made a large impact on our development process and has changed our approch to software development in our other projects and courses.
